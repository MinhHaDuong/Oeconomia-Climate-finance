# Nature Climate Change -- Analysis Article Format Specifications

Verified 2026-04-13 from NCC submission guidelines and the 2017 Brief Guide PDF.

## Content type definition

An Analysis is a new analysis of existing data or describes new data obtained
in a comparative analysis that leads to novel and arresting conclusions of
importance to a broad audience. Analyses have the same formatting as Articles
and are held to the same editorial and technical standards, but represent a
different kind of contribution: whereas Articles present a conceptual advance
to the literature, the value of an Analysis lies in the execution of the
analysis itself.

Analysis is classified as **primary research** (not Review/Comment/News & Views).

Source: <https://www.nature.com/nclimate/content>

## Word and element limits

| Element | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Main text | Up to 3,000 words (excluding abstract, online Methods, references, figure legends) |
| Abstract | Up to 150 words, unreferenced |
| Display items | Up to 6 (figures and/or tables combined) |
| Extended Data items | Up to 10 |
| References (main text) | ~50 (guideline, not hard cap) |
| Methods-only references | Separate from main text count |
| Online Methods | Up to ~3,000 words |
| Figure legends | < 300 words each |
| Title | 90 characters including spaces or fewer |

Source: <https://www.nature.com/nclimate/content>,
Brief Guide for Submission to NCC (revised 2017-02-22)

**Note on display items:** The 2017 Brief Guide listed 6 display items for
Articles. The current Content Types page lists 8 for Articles but 6 for
Analyses. The figure of **6 display items** for Analysis is consistent across
all sources checked.

## Title requirements

- 90 characters including spaces or fewer
- Avoid technical terms, abbreviations, punctuation, and active verbs

## Abstract requirements

- Up to 150 words
- Unreferenced (no citations in the abstract)
- Provide a general introduction to the topic and a brief non-technical
  summary of main results and their implications

## Manuscript structure

- Section headings should be used
- Subheadings may appear in "Results"
- Avoid "Introduction" as a heading (the introduction is unheaded)
- Methods section appears online only, after the main text

### Required sections/elements

1. Title
2. Authors (corresponding author marked with asterisk)
3. Abstract (150 words, unreferenced)
4. Main text with section headings (up to 3,000 words)
5. Online Methods (up to ~3,000 words, includes "Data Availability" and
   "Code Availability" subsections)
6. References
7. Acknowledgements (optional, brief)
8. Author contributions statement (required)
9. Competing financial interests statement (required)
10. Materials & Correspondence line
11. Figure legends

## Figure specifications

### Initial submission

- High-resolution files NOT required at initial submission
- Images must be of sufficient resolution for referees to assess the data
- Accepted formats: PDF, Word, or TeX/LaTeX (compiled PDF)

### Final (accepted) submission

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Resolution | 300 dpi minimum; 450 dpi recommended for highest quality |
| Color space | RGB |
| Typeface | Arial or Helvetica (same font for all figures); Symbol font for Greek letters |
| Font size | 5--8 pt optimum at print size |
| Panel labels | 8 pt bold, upright (not italic), lowercase a, b, c |
| Single-column width | 89 mm (final); ~90 mm (initial) |
| Double-column width | 183 mm (final); ~180 mm (initial) |
| Column-and-a-half | 120--136 mm |
| Maximum page depth | 247 mm (170 mm if legend underneath) |

### File formats (preferred order)

1. Vector (editable layers): .ai, .eps, .pdf, .ps, .svg
2. Layered raster: .psd, .tif (editable layered art)
3. Bitmap: .psd, .tif, .png, .jpg
4. PowerPoint: .ppt (fully editable, no styling effects)
5. Chemical structures: .cdx (ChemDraw)

### Color and accessibility

- Use distinct colors with comparable visibility
- Avoid red/green contrast
- Recolor to color-safe palettes (e.g., green + magenta) -- strongly encouraged
- Avoid rainbow color scale
- Do not flatten labels or scale/error bars onto images

### Multi-panel figures

- Each complete figure must be a single file upload
- Multi-panel figures: arrange all sub-parts (a, b, c) as one image file
- Do not upload panels individually

## Supplementary information

- Submit as a single combined PDF: text, then figures, then tables (in that order)
- Limit supplementary figures to 10 or fewer
- Tables longer than one page: provide as Excel or similar
- Supplementary information is not copy-edited

## References

- Numbered style (Nature house style)
- Each number = one publication
- Include the title of the cited article or dataset
- Methods-only references continue numbering from main text but are separate
  from the main text reference count guideline (~50)

## Cover letter

Optional but recommended. Should include:

- Context and importance of the work
- Why it is appropriate for the diverse readership of NCC
- Confidential information (conflicts of interest)
- Declaration of related work in press or submitted elsewhere
- Suggested or excluded referees
- Standard statement: "We confirm that this manuscript has not been published
  elsewhere and is not under consideration by another journal. All authors
  have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to Nature Climate
  Change."

The cover letter is NOT shared with referees.

Source: Brief Guide (2017), <https://support.nature.com/en/support/solutions/articles/6000245674-cover-letter>

## Preprint policy

- NCC (Nature Portfolio) **encourages** posting preprints on non-commercial
  servers (arXiv, bioRxiv, etc.)
- Posting a preprint is NOT considered prior publication
- Preprints posted on servers will NOT be taken into account when assessing
  novelty
- **Preprints may be cited in the reference list** of submitted manuscripts

### Version restrictions

| Version | Posting allowed? |
|---------|-----------------|
| Original submitted version | Any time |
| Accepted manuscript | 6 months after publication |
| Published (copyedited, journal format) | Never on preprint servers |

### Companion papers as preprints

Preprint-posted companion papers CAN be cited as references. However,
"unpublished meeting abstracts, papers in preparation and papers under review
or in press without an available preprint should not appear in the reference
list." Therefore: if the companion paper (e.g., technical report or data
paper) is posted as a preprint, it is citable. If it is merely "in
preparation" without a preprint, it is not.

Source: <https://www.nature.com/nclimate/editorial-policies/preprints-conference-proceedings>

## Publishing model and costs

NCC is a **hybrid journal** (subscription + Gold OA option).

| Route | APC |
|-------|-----|
| Subscription (traditional) | No charge |
| Gold Open Access (CC license) | GBP 9,390 / USD 12,850 / EUR 10,850 |

Analysis articles are primary research and thus eligible for either route.

Colour figures: free in the electronic version; colour charges may apply for
print. Many institutions have Springer Nature OA agreements that cover the APC.

**Note:** The subscription route carries no APC -- this is relevant given the
project's preference for diamond/no-fee OA venues. NCC subscription route is
free to the author but paywalled to readers.

Source: <https://www.nature.com/nclimate/our-publishing-models>,
<https://support.nature.com/en/support/solutions/articles/6000211135-article-processing-charges>

## Data and code availability

- Data Availability statement required in Methods under "Data Availability"
- Certain data types must be deposited in public structured repositories
  with accession numbers
- Other data encouraged in Dryad, figshare, or as supplementary information
- Code Availability statement required in Methods under "Code Availability"
- Custom code central to conclusions must be available to editors/referees
  on request; best practice is public release at publication

## Double-blind review

Available as an option. Authors must prepare the manuscript to conceal
identities and tick the appropriate box during submission. Editors do not
verify anonymization.

## LLM/AI policy

LLMs (e.g., ChatGPT) do not satisfy authorship criteria. Attribution of
authorship carries accountability, which cannot be applied to LLMs.
Authors must disclose AI tool use.
