## It is necessary to agree upon standards of expected ethical behavior

> The author, the journal editor, and the peer reviewer must abide by ethical standards. Our ethic statements are based on COPE’s Best Practice Guidelines for Journal Editors. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

## 1. Duties of editors

### Publication decisions

* The editor of the journal is responsible for deciding which of the articles submitted to the journal should be published, and is accountable for everything published in the journal.
* Based on reviewers’ reports and editorial evaluation, the editor may accept, reject, or request modifications to the manuscript.
* The editor must respect legal requirements (libel, copyright infringement, plagiarism).
* The editor may consult other editors or reviewers in the decision process. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Fair play

* Manuscripts must be evaluated solely on intellectual content. No discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, ethnic origin, citizenship, or political beliefs. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Confidentiality

* The editor and editorial staff must not disclose any information about a submitted manuscript to anyone other than the corresponding author, reviewers, or appropriate editorial advisors. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Disclosure and conflicts of interest

* Unpublished materials disclosed in a submitted manuscript must not be used by an editor in their own research unless they have the explicit written consent of the author. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

## 2. Duties of Reviewers

### Contribution to Editorial Decisions

* Peer review is meant to help the editor decide about publication; it can also help authors improve their papers. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Promptness

* If a reviewer feels unqualified or unable to review promptly, they must notify the editor and excuse themselves. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Confidentiality

* Manuscripts sent for review must be treated as confidential. They must not be shared or discussed with others except as authorized by the editor. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Standards of Objectivity

* Reviews must be objective. Personal criticism of the author is inappropriate. Critiques should be supported by arguments. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Acknowledgement of Sources

* Reviewers should point out relevant published work not cited by authors. Any claim that a result was previously reported must be accompanied by a citation.
* If the reviewer knows of substantial similarity or overlap with other published papers, they must alert the editor. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Disclosure and Conflict of Interest

* Privileged info or ideas from peer review must not be used for personal gain.
* Reviewers should recuse themselves if they have conflicts (e.g. competitive, collaborative, institutional relations). ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

## 3. Duties of Authors

### Originality and Plagiarism

* Authors must ensure their work is wholly original. If they use others’ work or words, they must cite or quote appropriately. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Multiple, Redundant or Concurrent Publication

* Authors should not publish substantially the same research in more than one journal.
* Submitting the same manuscript to multiple journals concurrently is unethical and unacceptable. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Acknowledgement of Sources

* Authors must properly acknowledge the work of others that influenced their work. Relevant publications must be cited. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Authorship of the Paper

* Authorship should be limited to those who made a significant contribution to the submitted article.
* The corresponding author must ensure that all co-authors have seen and approved the final version and agreed to submission. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest

* Authors must disclose any financial or significant conflicts of interest that might influence results or interpretation.
* All sources of financial support for the project should be disclosed. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Reporting standards

* Underlying data for quantitative statements must be accurately presented.
* The manuscript should provide enough detail and references so others can replicate or verify the work.
* Fraudulent or knowingly inaccurate statements are unacceptable. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])

### Fundamental errors in published works

* If an author discovers a significant error or inaccuracy in their published work, they are obligated to notify the journal editor or publisher promptly and cooperate to publish an erratum or addendum. ([OpenEdition Journals][1])
