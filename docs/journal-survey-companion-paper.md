# Journal survey: Companion methods paper target venues

Date: 2026-03-20

## Context

The companion paper presents a framework for detecting structural breaks
and polarization in scientific literatures using sentence-transformer
embeddings. It is a methods contribution validated on the climate finance
case study. Distinct from the data paper (which describes the dataset for reuse).

## Primary candidates

### Quantitative Science Studies (QSS)

- **APC**: $750 (ISSI members) / $1,200 (non-members)
- **Scope**: Quantitative studies of science, science indicators, scholarly communication
- **Open access**: Gold OA (MIT Press)
- **Data policy**: Must share data in public repository with DOI
- **Abstract**: max 200 words
- **Preprint**: Strongly encouraged (preprint each version)
- **Waiver**: Available via Eugene Garfield Foundation

**Fit assessment**: Excellent. QSS publishes methodological innovations in
scientometrics. The framework (sliding-window divergence, censored-gap variant,
seed-axis bimodality) is exactly the kind of contribution QSS readers value.
The journal was founded by the editorial board of Journal of Informetrics,
which published key related work (Bose et al. 2021, Poetto et al. 2023).

**Advantage over Scientometrics**: QSS is fully open access and has lower APCs.
The journal is newer (2019) but has strong editorial board and ISSI backing.

### Scientometrics (Springer)

- **APC**: Hybrid (subscription journal with OA option)
- **Scope**: Quantitative aspects of the science of science
- **Data policy**: Data sharing strongly encouraged, data availability statement required
- **Established**: Since 1978, h-index high, broad readership

**Fit assessment**: Good. The most established journal in the field. Our
related work section cites several Scientometrics papers. However, the
hybrid model means the paper might not be open access unless we pay the
OA surcharge (typically ~€2,000+ for Springer).

### Journal of Informetrics (Elsevier)

- **Scope**: Informetrics, scientometrics, bibliometrics
- **Data policy**: Standard Elsevier data sharing

**Fit assessment**: Good fit for the methodology, but Elsevier's
publishing model (subscription, high APCs for OA) is less desirable.
Also, the QSS editorial board originally came from JoI, creating a
potentially awkward submission dynamic.

## Recommendation

**Primary target: QSS.** Best scope match, fully open access, reasonable
APCs, preprint-friendly culture, and the ISSI community is the natural
audience for this framework.

**Fallback: Scientometrics.** If QSS rejects or requests changes that
don't fit, Scientometrics has the broadest readership and longest track
record.

## Submission timing

The companion paper depends on the data paper for the corpus description
(cross-reference). Two strategies:

1. **Sequential**: submit data paper first, then companion with data paper
   DOI. Clean dependency chain, but slower.
2. **Parallel**: submit both simultaneously, each cross-referencing the
   other as "companion manuscript, submitted." Faster but requires
   coordinating two review processes.

The data paper should go first because it establishes the dataset's
credibility independently of the methods paper's results.

## Key formatting notes for QSS

- Abstract: max 200 words
- Cover letter: max 300 words, comment on significance and scope fit
- Data: deposit in public repository with DOI (Zenodo)
- Preprint: post to arXiv or SSRN before submission
- No explicit word limit found, but typical QSS articles are 8,000-12,000 words
